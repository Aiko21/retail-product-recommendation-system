## Phase 2 — Dataset Audit

Date: 2026-06-26

### Objective

Validate dataset quality and identify potential issues prior to model development.

### Activities

- Loaded Online Retail dataset.
- Performed structural validation.
- Checked missing values.
- Evaluated duplicate records.
- Assessed data types and descriptive statistics.

### Findings

- Dataset contains 541,909 records and 8 features.
- CustomerID contains 24.93% missing values.
- Description contains 0.27% missing values.
- 5,268 duplicate rows were detected.
- Quantity and UnitPrice contain negative values, indicating potential returns or cancellations.
- Dataset covers 38 countries with 4,372 unique customers and 4,070 unique products.

### Decisions

- Customer records without CustomerID will be removed during cleaning.
- Duplicate rows require further investigation.
- Return transactions will be analyzed before cleaning decisions are made.
- Only valid customer-product interactions will be used for recommendation modeling.

### Business Insight

Recommendation systems depend heavily on customer-product interactions. Missing customer identifiers and invalid transactions may reduce recommendation quality if not handled properly.

### Lesson Learned

Early data auditing helps identify potential risks that may negatively impact recommendation performance and business outcomes.

## Phase 4 — Data Cleaning

Date: YYYY-MM-DD

### Objective

Clean the dataset to retain only valid customer-product interactions.

### Activities

- Created a working copy.
- Investigated missing values.
- Removed records without CustomerID and Description.

### Findings

- Records without CustomerID and Description were removed.
- A total of 135,080 rows were removed due to missing critical information.
- 5,225 exact duplicate rows were identified and removed.
- A total of 8,872 cancelled transactions were identified and removed.
- After removing cancelled transactions, no negative quantity records remained.
- This suggests that negative quantities were primarily generated by cancellation or return transactions.
- 135,080 rows with missing critical information were removed.
- The final dataset contains only valid customer-product interactions.
- The final dataset contains 392,692 valid purchase interactions

### Decisions

- Only records containing valid customer identifiers will be retained for recommendation modeling.
- Exact duplicate transactions were removed to prevent overestimation of customer-product interactions.
- Cancelled transactions were excluded because they do not represent successful purchases
- The cleaned dataset will be used as the master dataset for feature engineering and recommendation modeling

### Business Insight

- Duplicate interactions may inflate product popularity and generate biased recommendations.
- Learning from cancelled transactions may reduce recommendation quality and negatively impact customer experience
- Transaction cancellations and product returns are highly correlated in this dataset.
- Recommendation systems require reliable customer-product interactions. Data cleaning significantly improves recommendation quality and reduces noise.

## Phase 9 — Hyperparameter Tuning

Date: 2026-06-27

### Objective

Evaluate recommendation quality and determine the optimal recommendation size.

### Activities

- Split interaction data into train and test sets.
- Evaluated multiple Top-K configurations.
- Calculated Precision@K, Recall@K, and MAP@K.

### Findings

- Recommendation quality varies across Top-K values.
- Precision decreases as K increases.
- Recall increases as K increases.
- The highest MAP@K was achieved at K = 5.

### Decisions

- Top-K = 5 was selected as the optimal recommendation size.
- Precision@5 and MAP@5 will be used as primary evaluation metrics.

### Business Insight

Providing a smaller number of highly relevant recommendations is expected to maximize customer engagement and conversion rates.

### Lesson Learned

Recommendation evaluation requires balancing relevance and recommendation breadth.
